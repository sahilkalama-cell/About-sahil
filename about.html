Climate & Weather

Bettiah experiences a typical north-Indian subtropical climate: hot summers (April–June), a monsoon season with heavy rains (July–September), and cool, pleasant winters (December–February). The monsoon brings most of the annual rainfall and deeply influences agriculture and daily life.

Why Bettiah is notable

Bettiah is the administrative heart of West Champaran district. It is known regionally for its cultural heritage, historical significance, and agricultural contributions. The area is close to natural spots and wildlife reserves in the region, and local festivals and markets bring the community together, making Bettiah a lively place to grow up and visit.
